## statement
For every integer $n \ge 1$, let $T(n) = 1 + 2 + \cdots + n$ denote the sum of the first $n$ positive integers, with $T(1) = 1$. Then $T(n+1) = T(n) + (n+1)$ for all $n \ge 1$.

## proof
By definition $T(n) = \sum_{k=1}^{n} k$. The $(n+1)$-st positive integer is $n+1$, so
$$T(n+1) = \sum_{k=1}^{n+1} k = \left( \sum_{k=1}^{n} k \right) + (n+1) = T(n) + (n+1).$$

## intuition
Passing from T(n) to T(n+1) just appends the next integer, n+1.
