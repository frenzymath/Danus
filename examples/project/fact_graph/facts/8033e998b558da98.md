## statement
For every integer $n \ge 1$, $T(n) = \dfrac{n(n+1)}{2}$.

## proof
By induction on $n$. Base case: $T(1) = 1 = \frac{1\cdot 2}{2}$. Inductive step: assume $T(n) = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$. By the one-step recurrence (predecessor fact), $T(n+1) = T(n) + (n+1) = \frac{n(n+1)}{2} + (n+1) = \frac{(n+1)(n+2)}{2}$, which is the claimed formula at $n+1$. By induction the identity holds for all $n \ge 1$.

## intuition
Sum the recurrence with the arithmetic-series pairing 1+n, 2+(n-1), ...
